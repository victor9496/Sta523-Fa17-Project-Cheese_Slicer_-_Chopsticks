I have to tell you that I have been very impressed with life at the Greens of Pine Glen. Greens of Pine Glen had a reputation in Durham for being a rough place to live, ghetto and having gangs. Over the last couple years the community's reputation has changed considerably. A new manager named Marilee Mahan came here about three years ago. Since comming here I have seen no gang activity at all and I think this in due in part to the 9:30 curfew here. You are free to come and go from the community but there is no " hanging out" in the streets, on cars or in the stairwells. There is also a child rule that children under 8 need to be attended by a babysitter or parentwhile they are outside. When you used to drive through here there were young children all in the streets who would not even get out of the way when cars came through. Now the children seem to be controled and it makes this community a good place to live. The management office has a "no nonsense" attitude. The staff is always professional and the office is always clean.
