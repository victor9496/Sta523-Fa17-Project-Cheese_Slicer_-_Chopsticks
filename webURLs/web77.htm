I orignally posted this as a response to another reviewer, however, I have slightly edited and I am reposting as a separate entry. I have been living in New Haven since 2009 when Chercez (sp?)was the manager and Elizabeth was the Assistant Manager, and I must agree with the FIRST original post, that while New Haven is a decent place to live, it was MUCH MUCH better when it was managed by Ticon opposed to Bainbridge. In addition, with the outrageous hikes in the rent (when I moved in my 3 bedroom it was 995.00, opposed to the 1140.00 I pay now),to me it has become clear that the Bainbridge Management company wants a certain type of renter at New Haven and as a resident of almost four years, it pains me to say that I think the resident they are trying to market to is one that is not ethnic (take that any way you want) nor familiies with kids. I have watched so many of my ethnic neighbors (both black, and mexican) with young children, have to move (sometimes by their own choice, sometimes not) due to Bainbridge's hike in the prices and its sad. Especially, since New Haven was once an apartment complex that helped a lot of people that lost their homes due to the whole subprime predatory lending issue. If you disagree with me, just go to the web site and look at the pictures of the people when you first log on (that flash across the screen).....that will give you an indication of who Baibridge is targeting as residents. I just watched a family who I know just moved in at the beginning year pack up and move, and it was a family of Africans with a set of triplets I think, hey, maybe they only signed a six month lease right? Well, if they did sign a six month lease I can guarantee you when they went to renew the lease the price went up at least 100.00 or more and maybe more than that if they wanted another 6 months lease and not a 12 or 13 month. At any rate, I must give credit where credit is due and the on site management are the best that I have experienced and I have lived in many different apartments...I am not just saying that, because trust I have had my differences with them, but for the most part, I think they are fair, fair as one can hope for. I have no complaints about maintenance, 9 time out of 10, your maintenance is taking care of the next day if not the SAME day. The grounds are well kept etc... while a decent place to live, the units are OVERPRICED and TICON MANAGEMENT was much better than BAINBRIDGE. Overall, I can't say I would NOT recommend New Haven, just know what you are getting into. The issues I expressed with the new property management company may not be an issue for some. It is very evident by the rising prices (and yes, I know it is common for rent prices to increase based on market value, but under the new management company, the prices seem very extreme) and NO INCENTIVES for current residents to renew (i.e., carpet cleaning, painting of walls, money off first months rent of the renewed lease term, etc.) that Bainbridge Property Management is trying to implement their own version of gentrification at New Haven. Consider yourself informed.
