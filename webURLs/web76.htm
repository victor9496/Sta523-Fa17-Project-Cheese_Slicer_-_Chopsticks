I love Copper Mill. I've lived here with my boyfriend for almost 2 years. The location is great, being on the edge of Durham and within 5 miles of Chapel Hill. The interstate is within one mile, and the Southpoint Mall is one exit down the road. Harris Teeter is a couple blocks away from the complex, as is a great little bar/restaurant called City Beverage. Management have been nothing but professional, friendly, prompt, and service-oriented. Maintenance has fixed any problems quickly and without hassle. I seriously give them all 5 stars. The complex is very dog-friendly (there's a small fenced-in dog area; there is also a walking trail and several stations stocked with poop bags and bins) and I've met a ton of residents just from walking my dog. Everyone I've encountered has been incredibly friendly and respectful. In my opinion, Copper Mill has the best apartments for the price. If you go closer to Chapel Hill, you'll find similar apartments for a higher price. If you go further into Durham, you'll find out-dated apartments at a similar price. Copper Mill, just like it's location, strikes a perfect balance. Nice apartments at a decent price. I have a 2-bedroom on the main floor. It is spacious (the living room area is somewhat smaller than necessary, not bad though), has nice carpet and wood laminate floors in kitchen. The counters and appliances are all updated. I love the deck, it's large with a nice view and a nice-sized storage closet. There's also a large laundry room with washer and dryer. I have read from other reviews of people citing thin walls and noisy neighbors...for me, that couldn't be farther from the truth. Sometimes I wonder if i live here all alone because it is so quiet!! Rarely do I hear my neighbors. The pool area is gorgeous! There are gas grills (there are also campsite-like grills located by the basketball courts and at the end of the pond/walking trail area). We grilled like 80% of our meals last summer. There's a volleyball court, a tennis court, a basketball court, a fitness room. My only complaints are that the fitness center is not state-of-the-art as the complex advertises. Also, the basketball court can get a little noisy sometimes...but I am one to appreciate the sound of life. The garbage/recycling area, as one reviewer stated, is a far walk depending on where you live. But I'm not lazy and I have a dog to walk, so it's no bother. My last comment is that I love the landscaping and the way the complex is set back off the road in a little valley of sorts. It feels very rustic and laid-back, clean and safe. I've never felt unsafe..and if there are reports of suspicious activity, you can be assured that Copper Mill will immediately alert you of it (by email), just to warn you and give you tips for staying safe. (this has only happened twice, during the holidays, when a couple of cars were broken in to. The parking is usually very adequate. It happens about 2 or 3 times a month that I end up parking a little further away than I'd like. Sorry this is so long. I just love this place. I moved here from the midwest for a job that I now hate with a passion. But I love the weather and I love this apartment complex, so I'm sticking it out for now. Good luck!

Apartment complex location: Right near the complex is a shopping center that includes Harris Teeter s, which makes food shopping quick and easy.
