My wife, me, and our little dog lived in Southpoint Village for 2 1/2 years before purchasing a home. After spending a whole day looking at apartments in Chapel Hill my boyfriend and I stumbled upon Southpoint Village. It was about ten minutes until the office closed but we decided to go in and at least grab some information. When we walked in we were greeted by a young man named Jacob Truax. He was busy with two other clients but told us he would be with us shortly. When the other people left and Truax came over to help us we knew it was past closing time and didn't want to keep him after hours but he insisted that helping us was not a problem. Of all the people in all the leasing offices we talked with that day Mr. Truax was hands down the nicest, funniest, and most helpful. He stayed after hours, gave us the full tour and even looked up/found the city transit routes and bus times when I voiced a concern about the apartments being too far away from UNC. Right on the spot he emailed me the links to the transit information and even offered to walk us out to our car with an umbrella when it started raining. Not once did my boyfriend or I feel like we were being sold or pressured (as we had at almost all the other apartment complexes we looked at). The service Mr. Truax provided was nothing but genuine and professional. We were so glad we had ended our apartment hunt for the day at Southpoint with Mr. Truax and both left talking about how nice and personable he was compared to all the other people at all the other leasing offices. We were so impressed with Mr. Truax that we knew we needed to leave him and the apartment complex (which was beautiful and safe) a good review regardless of whether or not we decided to rent there. I would recommend this community to anyone based on the service we received from Mr. Truax as an example of how the staff and community would respond should something in your apartment need maintenance... However, I doubt you'd have any problems at all. The apartments and the grounds were beautiful/well maintained, everything was very high-tech and everything about the complex was clean.

I've lived here at Southpoint Village for several years, and I have often wondered if it could possibly get better. I assure you that it has gotten better year. The place was bought last summer by a new company, and the change in management/staff was a complete 180 degree turn from what it used to be. The guys in the office are so outgoing that they are more like neighbors/friends than the "management". The maintenance guys here are strangely friendly, and it is a pleasant change from past experiences. The exercise gym got updated which was great, and my neighbors are always respectful. This complex seems like it doesn't age. I think it's about 5 or 6 years old, but everything seems brand new. The courtyard area is really nice too. I've seen posts about noise from neighbors, but I've never heard a peep from anyone. I think like anywhere, you'll hear your neighbors from time to time if they are loud. Buy a house or get a top floor unit if you don't want to hear people--right? If you want a friendly, professional staff, great amenities, and the nicest apartment in the Southpoint area, this is your place.

Leasing

Southpoint Village Apartments in Durham, North Carolina offers outstanding access to the area's primary employment centers, upscale retail, and the renowned Research Triangle Park. Within 1 mile of the property is Interstate 40, which connects you to the beltline and I-540. The community is adjacent to the Triangle's premier, high-end shopping mall, The Streets at Southpoint, anchored by Nordstrom and Hudson Belk. Our apartment community offers a variety of one, two, and three bedroom garden-style apartments and townhomes. The apartments feature nine-foot ceilings, arched doorways, open lofts, in-home washer and dryer, and garage parking. Community amenities include a clubhouse, swimming pool, fitness center, business center, and more. Southpoint is a luxurious community and is proud to be directly accessible to both Duke and University of Chapel Hill's hospitals and universities.
