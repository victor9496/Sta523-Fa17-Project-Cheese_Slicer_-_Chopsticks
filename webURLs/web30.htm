I have lived at Garrett West for about a year and a half now---first, in a two-bedroom (Fab), then in a one-bedroom (Mod). I have been very happy living here, and plan to renew my lease again. The two-bedroom floor plan has physical separation between the bedrooms, which is great for roommates. The one-bedroom plan is plenty of space for one person. (For a couple, I would probably go with the larger one-bedroom with the sunroom.) Both plans have great storage space (closets, pantry, shelves provided). Both plans also have laundry included in-unit, and the one-bedroom's laundry area is nice and spacious. The kitchen is both plans is also well laid-out. (Note that the kitchen in the one-bedroom is identical in size to the two-bedroom's!) I really wish the stove was gas, though. The leasing process was a bit stressful both times I did it. (This may have been because, both times, I was coordinating the lease via phone and email while out-of-state.) Once you move in, the staff is friendly and accessible. The office is open during convenient hours to pick up packages, pay your rent, etc. The community is very well-maintained; periodic maintenance and repairs are done promptly and professionally by uniformed staff, many of whom are at Garrett West daily and thus easily recognizable. The residents include a lot of graduate (not undergraduate) students, but it is not just students---there is a good mix of people, including some people with kids. (And lots of people with dogs!) Overall, the complex is quiet but friendly. I have had very little issues with hearing noise from the neighbors. (I have lived on the top floor both times, though.) It is a great place to work from home. Garrett West is also convenient to everything---Duke, groceries, Target, and I-40. I really like living here and would recommend it to anyone, especially to graduate students.
