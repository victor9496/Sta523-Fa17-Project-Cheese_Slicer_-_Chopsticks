Altera North Pointe is a hidden gem in Durham and has something for everyone: students, families, young professionals, retirees, etc. You would never find the amenities and quality you get here at another complex or even a freestanding house in this area. I work from home so I definitely am around and about the complex a lot. A couple things that I think are important to point out: Location - I can walk to the grocery store (Costco/Kroger) in 15 minutes, it's very close to the Ellerbee bike trail for commuting downtown to work, and it is only a 5 minute drive away from the 9th Street area of downtown. Apartment - When I moved in, the paint was fresh, the carpet was new, and the appliances were brand new GE Energy Star (except for the dryer, since there is no such thing as an efficient dryer). Overall I am extremely pleased with the look and feel of the apartment, if you look at the model pictures online, that is what your apartment will look like. The kitchen could be bigger (I am in the 2 bedroom floorplan) but is okay with one person cooking at a time. Neighbors and Noise - Noise is not an issue for me - I know my neighbors have held several family gatherings below me but I have not heard any of them. I see many different ages, ethnicities, cultures and family structures in Altera. With a diverse community, I find that people are more open and respectful of one another. The diversity of its residents makes Altera a great place to live. Gym - The only amenity that I do not like is the gym. It is very small, and once you have more than two people inside, it gets hot and cramped. I don't think they perform the routine maintenance checks/fixes on the equipment, but the stuff is brand new, so it might be okay for a little while. Maybe one day they'll expand the gym, but Planet Fitness is right down the road and well worth the $10 if you like to work out. Staff: I have had the most interactions with Anthony and have noticed that he remembers everyone's name, which is awesome and really brightens your day. Maintenance has been quick to fix minor problems that come up. Whenever you pass one of the maintenance guys they're always very friendly, and I feel comfortable having them in my apartment when I'm not there. Security: Security is not a concern for me. I have seen some complaints on past reviews about the gates not working. It's true that every couple of weeks a gate will stop working and it either won't open, or be open all of the time. Some have complained that the communities across the street seem sketchy - I don't think that's true, but even if I did, I never see anyone walking around here during the day or at night who doesn't obviously live here. One thing I would love for Altera to fix: Give us the back entrance gate back! That would be so convenient for me. All of my friends and Uber drivers end up at the back entrance where the gate is broken.
