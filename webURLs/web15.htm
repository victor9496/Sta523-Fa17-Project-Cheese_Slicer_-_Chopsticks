I've lived here for over six months now. I can be a harsh critic but honestly, Southpoint Glen is the best apartment complex I've lived in especially for the price; this is my fourth apartment. I do plan on extending for another year, so fingers crossed I won't get dooked. For the price, you cannot beat the location, less than a mile and a half from Sothpoint mall, Kroger, Roses, several gastations, and 440. On the flip side, your only option for cable is Time Warner, they will say otherwise, but Dish/Frontier is impossible to be used without it violating one of their regulations. Within the first two days I saw the biggest roach I've seen since I've been in North Carolina, I heard they maybe some kind of huge wood roach (I'm creeped out just talking about it.) But I immediately bombed the entire apartment. I set off like four bombs in my one bedroom, 700 sq. foot apartment. Later I found the roach dead, and since have not had ANY other issues with unwanted critters. I know things like that can be a huge deterrent but I've found unless a building is almost brand new this will most likely be the case, had this been a regular or recurrent issue they would have gotten one star. I live near the car care area and dumpsters, so whomever, (and it's been several different people) feels as though it is okay to open all their car doors and blast Adele, or whatever's poppin' in the world today. It's a little frustrating. Maintenance rides around in their go carts all day, and just from my first weeks here, I felt they were rude, and unwelcoming. My non-emergency fix took about a week to be resolved. I had one maintenance guy stare down one of my female guests like she was a medium rare steak, at least one of them did this, and I don't see him around too much anymore. Hopefully I won't have to call them too much. When I first moved here there was a wonderful young lady here named Rikkia in the leasing office, she was great, seemed to know all the residents by name, always greeted you with a smile, polite, and pleasant. Well, there is new staff, not a fan, but I guess I only need to see them on the 1st, and if I have a package. But if you're like me, and you're just looking for a nice, safe place to lay your head down, and mind your own business, then this apartment is good for you. The area is diverse, I'd say 50% white, 40% black, and 10% -------- or other. I don't think anyone will feel uncomfortable because of lack of diversity. The apartment has a fireplace-if you don't mind using real firewood in an apartment, nice sized balcony, washer and dryer included, nice finishes (I love the shower head, but it doesn't move), and I had no major issues upon moving in, inside the apartment. Complex amenities include pool, dog park, grilling areas, and gym. The grounds are very well maintained. And I will be honest; I think they recycle here but no one ever explained to me the do's and don'ts; I'm still not real sure, but this is just a heads up. All in all I would recommend these apartments. Good luck in your apartment search! Update: I do not like the leasing staff. Why am I not receiving my packages, and why have several been sent back to sender with out me hearing about it. They can really do better. I'm very disappointed. They need to get rid a few people, particularly "the leasing staff manager." I had to take away a star, very unfortunate.

We enjoyed living here so much that we renewed our lease for another seven months after our initial 1-year lease even though the rent increased by 15%. Two months into our renewal, we got new upstairs neighbors that walked much more heavily than our previous upstairs neighbors. The resulting noise from a footstep is an extremely loud creaking sound that cuts through conversations and wakes us up from deep sleep (see youtube video below for a recording). I have been absolutely miserable for the past five months as I have not slept through the night without disruption since the upstairs neighbors moved in. The fact of the matter is that these buildings are old and were cheaply assembled. The only way to fix this problem is to gut the interior and put in a better floor structure. If you choose to live here, choose an upstairs apartment.
