2014 - 2015 Not Recommended Reviewed 08/03/2015
I lived at the Eastwood Apartments for a year because of the low rent and proximity to Duke. It was a mixed experience, so let me break this down into the good and the bad. The good: -As I said, the apartment was cheap- $555 a month. -The Eastwood apartments are located just a third of a mile east of Duke's East Campus. Being able to walk from the apartment to class in 10 minutes is a huge point in favor of the Eastwood Apartments (if you're a student, that is). -The Princeton Villas office staff are courteous and helpful when you call or email them. They respond promptly and are quick to send a maintenance person over if you need one. -The maintenance people are great too, at least in my experience (I've heard different things from other residents). Every time I needed them, they were there and they solved whatever problem I had. They're also nice guys and can be fun to talk to and joke with, for what it's worth. The bad: -The whole Eastwood Apartment building is in pretty bad shape. Maintenance staff told me it was built sometime in the 70's and it looks like there's been no renovation at all since then. Basically it looks like a sketchy motel from the outside. To make things worse, there's a frat house behind the building space in between is full of trash. Princeton Villas isn't directly to blame for this (the frat boys are) but that doesn't make living near old newspapers, discarded plastic bottles, paper cups, and even old mattresses any less unpleasant. Naturally, the garbage attracts flies, roaches, and other vermin so unless you're lucky enough to have a screen on your back window (which I wasn't) you can forget about opening it (not that the air out there is particularly fresh anyway). -The appliances in my apartment were old and some of them only partly functional. For instance, only two of the four heating plates on the stove top worked, and my toilet had to be replaced because water would leak out of the tank when you flushed it. Maintenance got me a new toilet, but that meant that I had no toilet and no running water for about four hours. -The plumbing in general is in bad shape. I've just mentioned my toilet but its worth adding that my shower drain barely worked (I'd be up to my ankles in water after 2 minutes in there), the drain on the kitchen sink, where you have to wash your food, is old, chipped, and disgusting and it had no stopper (I had to buy my own). Also, the trap under the bathroom sink was so old and thinned out that it broke through one evening and the space under the sink flooded with blackened water and gunk from the 1970's. Again, the maintenance guys are great and fixed it the next day, but even they admitted that the sink drain probably hadn't been cleaned or replaced since the building was built. -The walls are old, painted over many times, and full of holes and nails. -The windows are dirty on both sides, some have no screens, and the locking mechanism at the bottom is broken on two out of three of them. -Electric issues: One of the outlets in the apartment didn't work at all and a few of the others were so worn out that when you plug something in the plug falls right back out. Also, the lights in the apartment are old and inadequate. A light bulb was missing altogether from the bathroom light when I moved in, one of the two rooms has no lights at all, and the light above the kitchen counter is so bad that it seems like the room actually gets darker when you turn it on, which I rarely did. My advice is to go to home depot and buy a standing lamp or two to brighten things up. -Roaches: Several roaches made it into my apartment, even though I kept it very clean. When I called the front office about this, they told me that the roaches (which they prefer to call 'water bugs') live on the roof and come up through the drains when it rains. In fact, I discovered that they come in through the back window, which is right above where all the garbage is. Their entry could be prevented by installing a screen there but instead Princeton Villas hires an exterminator who (according to the maintenance people) shows up quarterly to spray the apartments. I've attached a picture of the notice that gets put on the door to when this happens. That particular notice claims, inter alia, that the need for pest treatment in the building had 'reached emergency levels", that the exterminator will enter on November 6th, and that all furniture has to be moved away from the walls. It turned out, however, that the exterminator entered on the 7th, that it was NOT an emergency, and that furniture didn't have to be moved. All of this is depressing testimony to the organization and communication skills of the management folks. I was in the apartment for another 8 months after that, and the 'quarterly' insect treatment never took place again. -Finally, that the apartment is cheap is good, but the pricing is actually not competitive. I now live in an apartment nearby that has hardwood floors rather than the carpeting at Eastwood, clean walls, new appliances, new, clean windows (screens on all of them!), ceiling fans, far better lighting, and a new, clean bathroom. For all that, I pay only $90 more per month. Overall: The people are all very nice and helpful, but the building and units are in bad shape and need major renovation. My advice is to look elsewhere, even if you're on a budget, like I am. There are far better places available in the same price range.
