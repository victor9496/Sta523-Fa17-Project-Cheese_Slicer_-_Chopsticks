I lived at Artisan in a 1 bedroom with a 70 pound pet for a year, and I think there are pros and cons to this community. PROS: -Apartments are very nice for the area, with faux plank flooring and faux granite counters, track lighting, higher-end appliances, and deep sinks. I also never heard a peep out of my neighbors above me or beside me. Not a complaint at all here. -Staff are very friendly and courteous, and monthly events are planned. Plus, FOOD TRUCK WEDNESDAYS! -Grounds are kept orderly and pleasant. -Perhaps the best pool, grilling area, pet park, and gym in the area. Also ample sidewalks throughout the surrounding neighborhood for walking a dog. -Open floor plans CONS: -Location! Brier Creek is 10 minutes away, and that's as close you're getting to any grocery stores/shops/etc. -Prices are steep. While the apartments are pretty nice inside, you can get way cheaper in Durham or north/west Wake County. I was told I was getting a 'deal' because they waived the 'pool-view fee,' which is wholly unnecessary. They also wanted to raise my rate by $55 if I resigned my lease; not a very good marketing plan. Unless you plan on using all of their nice amenities, it's really not worth it. -The floor plans generally do not allow for a lot of natural light, and with the darker flooring and cabinets, the apartment tends to be pretty dark. -Dog owners, be careful of your dog's claws scratching up the flooring. Also, like most places, steep non-refundable pet fee and $15 pet rent is a drag. Overall, it's a nice, well-managed complex in a nice-looking neighborhood, but is pretty out of the way and you can find way better pricing for almost as nice apartments elsewhere in the area. Unless you plan on using all of the amenities often and you need an apartment that looks more luxurious than it is, it's probably not worth it.
