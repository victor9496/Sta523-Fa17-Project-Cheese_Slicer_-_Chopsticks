2013 Recommended Property Reviewed 06/10/2013
The Lofts is a great place. I'm currently in a six month lease and have a 2 year assignment in the Triangle. The Duke Med area is too far away from the things I like, but had they moved this apartment complex closer to where I want to be I'd stay here without question. Noise is rarely a concern. I barely hear anyone and there are people both above and below me. Occasionally I will hear a small amount of noise but it's minimal. The "fit and finish" of the unit is good. The windows are bigger than the other places I visited in North Hills and Downtown Raleigh, so more light is let in the unit. Hardwoods and tile on the floors - painted accent walls - honestly you won't find a better detailed place for the money. The exercise room is good as is the rest of the common area. They have two pools and at least one will be quiet so if you want to go out and relax you can. A couple of OK restaurants within walking distance and downtown Durham is less than a 5 minute car ride - if that. With the Duke Campus so close you can always find a pleasant bike ride. I wouldn't say the area is "walk-able" for a night on the town but it is certainly "ride-able" if you enjoy biking. You are near the edge of a less affluent area but there has never been a time I was worried at all for my safety. Staff has always been good and the maintenance folks are nice. This is a nice compromise. Very quiet, well constructed and detailed, and close (just not in the middle) of things. If you want to live in Durham and be close, but not "in" downtown - and ESPECIALLY if you work at the hospital - you won't find a better place.

2012 - 2013 Recommended Property Reviewed 02/12/2013
I have lived at The Lofts for just under a year and have been impressed with the quality of the building, the amenities, and the staff. I'm guessing the staff must have changed since the previous reviews, as I have found all of them to be sweet, helpful, and courteous. There is plenty of parking in the garage. Upon moving in, it is stated explicitly that parking in the areas across the street in the business spaces or in reserved spaces will result in towing. There is a visitor space in the front and back of the building, plus you can always buzz in guests to the garage if needed. The noise is typical of apartment life. Mostly quiet, given the older grad students and medical residents, but every now and then a bit louder when Duke plays or someone hosts a party. Nothing extreme. The grounds are safe and gated. Maintenance is prompt and thorough. The amenities, including gym, 2 pools, business center, and social activities, are well maintained and enjoyable. The Lofts is pricier than other apartments, but it is one of the only luxury apartments in Durham, and you do get what you pay for. I highly recommend living here.
