2012 - 2015 Recommended Property Reviewed 05/14/2015
I have never been the type to write online product reviews, or the like, and have never written a review for any apartment complex I've ever lived in, but I have also never had as positive a living experience as the one I have had here. I moved to Legacy at Meridian in Durham, NC (by the Southpoint mall) 3 years ago to attend law school at the University of North Carolina, and the place immediately felt like home. Tina, who manages the property, went above and beyond to secure my apartment for me (all over the phone), and the transaction and move-in were as seamless as I've ever experienced (and I moved in on a weekend!!!!!!!!). I had a tee time I needed to make that day, so Tina and the staff worked with me to get my lease paperwork signed, and the keys in my hands in less than 15 minutes. And, things only got better from there. I lived in a 1 bedroom apartment (unit 504), right beside the pool and clubhouse, and instantly fell in love with the place. The apartment complex boasts a diverse array of residents, ranging from graduate students and young professionals, to families and retirees--all of whom I have found to be very friendly (and normal)--sharing the same sentiments that I will be sharing here. Being that I had taken some time off between college and law school, I was looking for a place that offered me a quiet atmosphere for studying, but also provided a fun, social atmosphere when I was able to get some time away from the books, and Legacy at Meridian did not disappoint. This place really offered the perfect balance between fun, and professionalism--mainly due to the environment the staff strives to provide, and the residents that make Legacy what it is. **And as a disclaimer, I should say that as a 28 year old, I was not too concerned with this facet, but this place is definitely child/family friendly, and I see kids enjoying themselves in the pool and the courtyard all the time.** When I moved in, the apartment complex actually went by the name "1520 Magnolia," and Tina handled the transition to the new management company flawlessly. Since the change, there have been a ton of HUGE improvements to the premises and amenities (including new pool furniture, a completely renovated clubhouse, re-painted and re-finished apartment buildings, and nonstop landscaping improvements). On top of that, Tina and the new management company have helped make this place 'ahead of the curve' by helping to build a social media presence, and offering residents "Food Truck Wednesdays" where the management company arranges for food trucks to come to the apartment complex each Wednesday evening (this, in my opinion, was the single greatest improvement). Not only has it allowed me to meet people living here I may not have otherwise had the chance to, but it's also a great, quick meal, that everyone enjoys immensely. I am honestly SHOCKED that anyone would have anything but the most positive things to say about this place, or the people working here. Each and every person working in the leasing office is willing to go above and beyond the call of duty to make sure your needs are fulfilled in as timely a manner as possible--and they all do it with a genuine smile on their face, demonstrating that they are not just helping you for your business, but because they actually care about doing the right thing, and care about helping make your living experience the way it should be. They are very professional, but in a friendly manner that makes you feel like far more than a paying resident, or a client. Every maintenance person I've had work on anything in my place has been very professional, has fixed everything I've needed fixed expeditiously, and has gone out of their way to be friendly (both when completing work orders, and just crossing paths around the complex). The pool is absolutely gorgeous. I think it is far better than Alta, Meadowmont, and all the other Chapel Hill apartment's pools--and is also much nicer (with a far more enjoyable atmosphere) than any of the other apartment complexes in the Southpoint area. There is plenty of seating, and I've never had an issue with the pool over-crowding. And, you will never encounter any situation where people who do not live here (college kids) are over-flooding the pool, EVER--though, of course, you're welcome to bring guests with you to the pool. The fitness center was also renovated when management was improving the complex, which has saved me a lot of time when I have been in a rush, and unable to make it to the gym to work out. Every morning, there is breakfast food and coffee available in the clubhouse, and I used to take advantage of that every single morning. And, management even goes out of their way to throw soirees out by the pool, or in the clubhouse, from time to time (whether celebrating a holiday, or simply as a thank you to us), and they will provide food, drinks, and will hire someone to play live music, too. I've lived in some nice places over the years, but c'mon...seriously....anyone with half a brain who's reading this and comparing what I am saying with the rent I know they charge has only one conclusion to come to, which is why I am sharing that here--despite my doing so going against my very nature. In closing, I will simply say that I was incredibly lucky to stumble upon this place 3 years ago when doing a random Google search for apartments, and I know that if I return to the triangle area, Legacy at Meridian is where I would want to live. Whether you are here for school, or for work, I am sure you would be very pleased with your decision to live here. Tina, Tom, Monica, and all of the staff here are very nice, and helpful, so if you are looking for a place to live, and what I've described sounds like it may be up your alley, ask for one of them and I'm sure they will help you as they have me these past 3 years.
